Having Cramps?


Abdominal cramps are the most frequent menstrual complaint. Unlike many other symptoms, which begin 1 to 2 weeks before your period and end when bleeding starts, cramps usually show up right before show time and last for 2 to 3 days
CONTROL MEASURES
1.Hot water remedy(bath and drink hot water) 2.Take ginger drink

Having headaches?


Changes in estrogen levels are to blame if you experience headaches leading up to your period. If you’re prone to migraines, you’ll probably find that you get them before your period.
CONTROL MEASURES
(1)Cold therapy:Wrap an ice pack in a towel and apply it to your forehead (10 minutes on, 10 minutes off). Cold therapy can reduce inflammation and dull the sensation of pain.
(2)Relaxation exercises:Exercises like meditation, yoga, and deep breathing can relax your muscles, reduce tension, and improve headache symptoms.

Fatigue is a vicious cycle for many women at this point in their cycle. Shifting hormones make you tired, but they also disturb your sleep patterns.
CONTROL MEASURES 1.Stay hydrated(Drink alot of water ) 2.Avoid heavy meals and caffeine at night 3.Add probiotic like yoghurt to your meals
